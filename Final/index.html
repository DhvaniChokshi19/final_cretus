Our Projects

Chess Playing Robot

It is a robotic arm with a brain of its own which can play chess like any human can do with another robot or human.

Features

  • A Brain - Brain is a system for thinking which moves should the robot play and how should the robotic arm execute it.

  • A robotic arm - Robotic arm is the mechanical system which just looks like a human arm and will move the chess pieces on board as per the commands given by the brain.

  • A sensory chess board - Sensory board is a chess board fitted with sensors for getting information of the location of all pieces on the board.

Self Balancing Robot

A Jetson Nano-powered, two-wheel self-balancing robot with a 3D-printed chassis, showcasing remarkable water stability for versatile exploration. Its AI-enabled capabilities and real-time data processing empower intelligent decision-making in challenging environments.

Features

Advanced self-balancing technology ensures smooth navigation on various terrains.Customizable 3D-printed chassis allows easy integration of sensors and payloads. Water-stability has been achieved during its self balancing . AI-driven intelligence empowers real-time data processing and intelligent decision-making.

Events Organized

Arduino 101

The Club organized an engaging workshop titled "Arduino 101" with the aim of introducing participants to the basics of Arduino and providing hands-on experience in working with Arduino boards and sensors. The workshop was divided into two parts, covering the fundamental concepts and components in the first half, and offering practical experience through hands-on projects in the second half.

Arduino 101

Robosoccer

The event aimed to showcase the creativity and technical skills of the participants while providing an exciting and competitive environment. The tournament consisted of multiple games, with each game lasting approximately 2 minutes. The player with the highest number of goals in each game emerged as the winner and received a reward of a 50 Rupees coupon for a stationary shop.

Competitons Participated

Technoxian

The competition is organized by Robotech Labs Pvt. Ltd. in association with AICRA and Ministry of Science & Technology, Government of India. A team from Cretus participated and built a Maze Solver Robot. The competition served as a platform to showcase their skills and gave them a chance to experience a great exposure with teams around the world.

Technoxian

Robofest

Robofest is a festival of competitions and events organized by Gujarat Council on Science and Technology. This year three teams from the club participated and built rover, warm drones and self-balancing robot. Being one of the finest Robotic festivals our teams faced a tough competiton from all over India but managed to clear the first round.

Robofest

AriaRo, the Mega Technical Symposium organized by Karnavati University became a platform for our team to showcase their skills of Computer Vision building an Object Tracking Camera. The team was introduced themselves in the front of the authorities and represented our Club and our University.
